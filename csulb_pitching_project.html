During my time at California State University, Long Beach I, along with my master's chair Sam Zeff, PhD, collected motion capture data from the entire CSULB pitching staff. I created a MATLAB script to process and create customized reports like the ones above, along with 3-D videos with live timeseries data highlighting key metrics important to each pitcher. We consulted with coaches, athletic trainers, and strength coaches to help design individualized training programs for each pitcher.

More details on this project

This project was my first exposure to sports biomechanics research, and was where I first fell in love with sports biomechanics/performance science. Being able to speak with former MLB coaches and players to get their input on what information was valuable and relevant helped me to hone my translational skills. Collaborating on training schemes with a multitude of individuals with diverse backgrounds and responsibilities helped me to understand how to work in a mulitfaceted team.
